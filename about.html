THE BOBOLA LEGACY

It started with a 13-year-old dishwasher who fell in love with the work.

Frank Bobola started in the restaurant business at 13 as a dishwasher. He worked his way up to chef at places like The Waterwheel, Branding Iron Steak House, The Colonial Inn, and The Lakeside. In the mid-80s he opened Big Belly Deli in Lowell, which grew into Bobola's Big Belly Deli & Restaurant on Gorham Street, a true neighborhood spot.

On March 19, 2018, the family opened this location at 9 Simon Street in Nashua. Frank passed before he got to see it open, but everything we do here still carries his belief that good food and good people belong together.

Eight years later, we're not just looking back. We're proud of the food coming out of our kitchen today and the family running the place now. Keeping Frank's legacy alive doesn't mean standing still. It means carrying his standards forward and making this place even better for the neighbors who count on us.

What Frank taught us: Treat people right. Give them more than they expect. Keep the recipes simple and the portions generous. The rest takes care of itself.

This is what we actually believe.

We put the money in the portions.

You won't find tiny plates with microgreens here. You get a real meal that fills you up and makes you want to come back. That's how we were raised.

Come as you are.

Work boots still dusty? Kids in tow? Just want a quiet cup of coffee? You're our people. No dress code, no attitude. Just a warm hello and a full plate.

We're the neighborhood place.

Not trying to be the "it" spot. We're the place you bring your cousin when they're in town, the place you go after a long week because you know it'll be good and the people will remember your name.

OUR JOURNEY

From dishwasher to Nashua staple.

1970s
Frank starts as a dishwasher at 13. Discovers his life's work.
1980s
Opens Big Belly Deli on Westford Street in Lowell. Becomes a neighborhood institution.
2018
Bobola's opens at 9 Simon Street, Nashua on March 19, 2018. The family carries the legacy forward.
TODAY
Still family-run and generous, and always evolving. New dishes, the same welcome, and a kitchen we're proud of. The next chapter is about the food and the family carrying it forward.

About the chef on our logo

The original logo was drawn by Frank's daughter Kimberly years ago, a little cartoon that became the face of the place. It was warm, a little quirky, and felt like family.

We've given him a fresh coat of paint for this chapter, but the spirit is the same: friendly, a little fun, always welcoming. He reminds us why we do this, to make people feel at home the second they walk in.
